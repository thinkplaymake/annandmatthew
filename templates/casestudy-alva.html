Hire people, not CVs.
positioning and category engagement for startup redefining hiring

Alva Labs were at a pivotal point in their company history - lots of success, lots of plans, but not happy with how they were introducing themselves to the world - so we stepped in to help.

The Brief

Alva Labs AB is a Stockholm based SaaS scale-up, providing psychometric tests based on the Big 5 Model of Personality.

Working from a basis of validated, peer-reviewed academic research, the Big 5 comprises a better way to screen early-stage job applications.

Knowing they needed to launch content marketing to engage potential customers, increase retention and create thought leaders to market their value proposition

The Friction

Many of their leadership team have PhDs, or are licensed psychologists; they came to Alva straight from academia; successful scientists evidence every point you want to make, do not exaggerate and balance every claim. You are calm, measured and reasoned. This is not the mindset that will help a start-up to solve the problems they see.

This mindset was seen in their existing style guidelines: they were strict, academic and terse. No-one felt empowered to write.

The team were all writing and working in a second language which, even for the most conversant expert, can add an additional layer of self-doubt and hesitation.

We needed to communicate the validity and superiority of the Alva Labs assessment over its non-scientific peers, the Meyers Briggs & DISC, locate our writing confidence and share our ideas. Trash-talking the competition was never going to be our strategy, and we needed to integrate warmth and wit, and bring a new confidence to a team of superlative talent

OUR APPROACH

The founders were wrapped up in too many versions of their story - none of which they felt were right. So we took a step back, and asked Claudia and Ulrika tell their own story in their own words.

We located the true problem they’re solving. We helped them rediscover their purpose, and turned that into a clear positioning for their business.

The result?

Ulrika and Claudia, beaming. Two founders saying “Yes, that’s us”.
